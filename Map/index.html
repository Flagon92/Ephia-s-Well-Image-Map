The Krak des Roses

Headquarters of the Cinquefoil Rose

Description

The Cinquefoil Rose is a coalition of the forces that once fought tooth and nail to free Ephia's Well from Orentid occupation. Originally composed of refugees and heroes from far-away places, it now exists as a populist movement and formal alliance of the occupants of the Krak des Roses. Crucial to the citadel's defense, and bearing a never-quite-suppressed enmity for Baz'eel and her Janissaries, the Cinquefoil Rose blooms still in Ephia's Well... thorns and all.

"We the swift remember to ye the dead, all the red blood that beats in our hearts."

Notable Features

  • College of Balladeers of the Lost Heart: The Balladeers of the Lost Hearth still follow the noble ideals of the old Rose. Originally the squires, troubadours, servants and sons of the original Cinquefoil Rose they have banded together and do all they can to keep the legacy of their fallen forebears alive. They are forever seeking a prophesied hero who shall claim "the Cup," the mystical relic whose waters might reinvigorate the arid sands.
  • Fortress of the Banda Rossa: The Banda Rossa are a mercenary company under exclusive contract with the Grandmaster of the Cinquefoil Rose. They serve as the bulk of the military might of the Knights of the Cinquefoil Rose.
  • The Sisters of the Sibylline Vine: A monastic community of mystics, seers, fanatics and scholars united by shared consumption of a sacred wine, called "the Drink" by laymen. This wine is said to grant them visions of things that have yet to pass. They serve as the mystic foundation of the cause.

Janissary Garrison

Headquarters of the 4th Legion of Janissaries

Description

The IVth Legion of the Sultan's Janissaries has been given the dubious privilege of maintaining law and order in Ephia's Well. Called "the Misfortune" by their compeers in Baz'eel, they are dominated by an obsession with their perceived ill luck. Carrying charms and following their own superstitions, they prowl the Well looking for ne'er-do-wells... and "ensuring" they find a fair few each time.

Unfortunately for them, they are also the Well's first line of defense against invasion, and many find themselves on the wrong end of a barbarian's axe, or dragged to Qa'im to suffer a fate worse than death.

"Toss the ash..."

Notable Features

  • 'The Misfortune': The 4th Legion is plagued by a legendary string of bad luck, and is looked down upon by the other Legions of the Sultan as the runt of the litter. Regardless, the soldiers of the 4th go about their duty with grim determination.
  • Tossing the Ash: A common superstition amongst the soldiers of the 4th. Many of them carry pouches of ash on their belt, to toss over their shoulder in order to drive bad luck away. Many in the 4th also consider Urazzir, The Wroth, as their patron god in the hopes that he will drive misfortune away from them.

Eagle's Mount

Headquarters of the Astronomers of Q'tolip

Description

The Astronomers of Q'tolip are the free-thinking, eccentric collection of scholars gathered under the banner of the enigmatic and brilliant visionary, Q'tolip. Seeking to master the cosmos through scholarship, they are constantly engaged in the quest for knowledge of yore, and indulge in the power that comes from such things.

In more practical terms, they are the primary archaeologists and magical authority of Ephia's Well, and their maintenance of the Shade affords them influence that few other organizations can boast. Often, the quest for knowledge is hindered by the unlearned, and the Astronomers understand well that their search can only be strengthened by cultivating a safe environment for their studies to bear fruit.

"As above, so below."

Notable Features

  • The Celestial Disc: The Astronomers believe that the moon, or the Celestial Disc, is the fulcrum of our Universe. All rules which anchor the soul draw from it, and not from elsewhere. All others are mistaken, and all other readings are untrue.
  • Q'tolip the Exile: The Astronomers have their origins in the Grand Academy of Baz'eel, and Q'tolip was once a respected lecturer there. Following his revelation - that the Universe revolves around the Celestial Disc, the Moon, and that all other universal constants must be so re-ordered, he was expelled from the Academies - and the Astronomers are those who followed him into exile.
  • The Shade: Or the Nasaru, is the means by which the Astronomers proved their worth to the Assembly. A mysterious magical artifact rumoured to have been crafted by the giants themselves, it serves as a protective shell against the Ash, keeping the citadel of Ephia's Well safe from the deadly storms that plague the waste.

The Pyramid of Ephia's Well

Seat of the Government and home to the Assembly

Description

Ephia's Well is a small-scale fortress and sometimes-settlement that has long occupied a prominent and central spot on the trade routes between the splendorous cities of the desert, straddling a crossroads of the great Giant's Road. It is said to have been built by the ancient giants thousands of years ago; now their ruinous walls form the innermost quarter of the fortress, surrounding a bountiful oasis that flows outwards from tributaries deep within the earth. The surrounding walls have been built and torn down by conquerors, adventurers, and merchants... again and again and again.

The most recent incarnation of these attempts at state-building is a small colony-town tucked within the resurgent Sultanate of Baz'eel. It is governed by an assembly of Legates elected from among the Voiced population (those true citizens of the Well who have paid for their right of citizenship) who reign on behalf of the Sultan.

"Our walls, they crumble. Our people, they starve. We must build. We must build a jewel in the desert, a rival to all who behold it..."

Political Leagues

Many of the Legates are drawn from the three most prominent political Leagues of Ephia's Well:

  • The League of Purple: Staunch traditionalists who advocate for the sanctity of the Wheel faith and the traditions of the Sultanate.
  • The League of Gold: Who value coin over blood. They foster the growth of legitimate enterprise, and seek out trade and riches from the desert beyond.
  • The League of White: Who position themselves as voice for the poor and needy. The seek to give voice to the Voiceless, and ensure that no man goes hungry or unpaid.

Notable Features

  • The Hall of Governance: The room where an Assembly formed by the Voiced citizens of the Well gather, and the laws of the Well are chiseled into magical Stele of Law. It is host to many a fierce debate.
  • The Scribes of the Sublime Garden: The unceasingly busy bees of the Well, the commendable and highly stressed civil servants of the government. They are constantly collecting information, ensuring that letters arrive on time, and generally serving at the leisure of Ephia's legates. They are the workhorse of the minutiae of governance, and they are forever ensuring that taxes are paid, citizens are registered, and food is stored. Their duties and responsibilities are exhaustive. They, too, are exhausted. But the work is never done.

The Cistern

Where the Waters of the Well gather

Description

This cavernous and humid chamber built under the Well's main plaza is where the mythical Waters emerge from the depths and are stored. Here, the Scribes of the Sublime Garden perform the laborious task of portioning the Well's bounty and distributing it to those who are entitled to a share. Watchful Janissaries protect the Cistern, for its corruption would surely spell doom for all who live in the citadel.

Housed within are the statue of the Pilgrim, which pokes its head and shoulders above the plaza, and the statues of Kneeling Giants, which appear to bow in obeisance to it.

"Spread the waters; its channels and tributaries are arteries of the world."

Notable Features

  • Statue of the Pilgrim: This enormous sandstone statue is of a hooded and androgynous figure clasping a bowl or cup. Like other statues of giants that litter the Citadel of Ephia’s Well, it is very old and certainly greatly predates the more recent constructions. It is popularly known as “the Pilgrim” and stretches from some unknown depth to rise above the center of the Citadel’s Plaza. A grove has been cut into it so as to allow the waters of the well to spill from the bowl and into a pool. There are several conflicting legends about what it is intended to depict...
  • The Kneeling Giants: These sand scoured and weathered figures of tremendous size, kneeling upon the sand, can be found in and around the Well. It is unclear whether they kneel in respect or defeated submission.”
  • The Holy Waters: Ephia’s Well is a satrapy of Baz’eel, and it provides the water that Baz’eel so greatly needs and desires. This strange substance makes Ephia's Well a place worth fighting over. A requirement for potion-making and life-living alike. Its magical properties can close wounds and heal ailments in a heartbeat. The Waterbearers of B'aara hold the Waters to be the tears of the goddess herself, come to reinvigorate the world, though many question the truth of this statement.

The Souk of Salt and Spices

The smell of spice and the sounds of commerce...

Description

One of the settlement’s greatest pride and wonder, the Souk of Salt and Spices is the marketplace of Ephia’s Well and the beating heart of its commerce. Dinars flow as freely as water within the Souk, with merchants touting their wares from overflowing stalls while stern-faced Janissaries watch for any thieving hands.

Anything, it is said, may be bought and sold at the Souk, be it openly or in pipeweed-shrouded dens, from wondrous artifacts to lost relics of Old Formoria to barrels of water destined for far-flung settlement desirous of the Well’s ever-flowing bounty.

"Open Air Pork Falafel! Accept no substitutes!"

Notable Features

  • The Temple of the Mother: Within the Souk sits the Temple of the Mother, B’aara, welcoming the huddled masses yearning for a drop of precious water, oft more valuable than dinar.
  • The Stadium: The Bloodsands, the stage on which great deadly gladiatorial games are held in honor of the Well's illustrious visitors, and on religious occasions, in honor of Agaslakku, the Warrior.

The Tablet

Home to the Stonefolk, Dwarves and the destitute.

Description

The Tablet is, strictly speaking, a ghetto beyond the gates of Ephia’s Well yet guarded by the shadows of its walls. There, a substantial population of Stonefolk and dwarves reside, along with those who either chaff at or have been made unwelcomed within the citadel.

From fencers of stolen goods to unlicensed archaeologists, to merely the impoverished and the destitute, the Tablet absorbs those whom the Well itself could not come to shelter or they who refuse to accept the promised sanctuary of the Well.

"Welcome you are, and also welcome I give to you."

Notable Features

  • The Stonefolk: The Stonefolk are tall, muscular creatures with brown or pale-grey skin. They have no irises, and their hair exhibits as hard, thin strands. They are a people of uncertain provenance, who have long forgotten their origins.

    They serve as stonemasons and labourers, else wander in small groups in the company of their groknaks.

    Stonefolk culture is orally transmitted, and their praise-singers are the living embodiment of their culture. While not unintelligent, they have no patience for book learning and are slow and considered in all things. They have a distaste for violence and warfare.
  • The High Dwarves: Gold Dwarves, otherwise known as High Dwarves, are a race of Dwarves that descend from the civilisation of Mount Kulkund, in the north of the Great Ash Desert.

    High Dwarves are exiles from their ancient home, and many long to reclaim it for their people from the Deep Dwarves who defeated and banished them.

    High Dwarves have a cultural taboo against charity, and consider it a grievous insult both to themselves and to their Ancestors, whom they venerate with an almost-religious respect. Gold Dwarves are naturally suspicious of people who use flattery and 'polite manners'; even saying 'please' and 'thank you' could be seen as cunning manipulative behaviour by a Gold Dwarf.
